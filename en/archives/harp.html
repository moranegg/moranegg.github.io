Biography

Morane Otilia Gruenpeter (b.1987) started playing the harp at the age of 9 with Mariana Schwartzbart and worked with her continuously until the achievement of the B.MUS diploma at the Jerusalem Rubin Music and Dance Academy. Morane continued her studies with Germaine Lorenzini in France, until January 2012. Morane participated in master classes with renowned harpists such as Catherine Michel, Isabelle Perrin, Marie-Pierre Langlamet Florence Sitruk, Marielle Nordmand and Sivan Magen.

Morane is an America-Israel Culture Fund scholar from 2001. Morane participated in the "144 String Quartet", a Celtic harp quartet, which was formed in 1999 that participated in the 10th year anniversary of "Les Journées de la Harpe" in Arles France in October 2004, issued a disk in the summer of 2005 and performed at the opening of the International harp competition in Israel in the Foyer in 2006 and 2009. In 1999 she received the third prize in the International competition for chamber music in Arles France "Concours d`Arles 1999" for "Duo de Harpes Celtiques“ In 2008 she participated at the 10th WHC (world harp congress) in Amsterdam as part of Pierick Houdy's master class on his Sonate for harp. Morane was nominated as an accomplished musician during her military service, which allowed her to continue her music studies. Morane has founded in 2010 with her harp colleagues the IHS (Israeli harpists society) an non profit organization which its goal is to promote Harp culture in Israel.
